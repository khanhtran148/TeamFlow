### Summary

The two most critical issues are identical N+1 patterns in `GetBacklogHandler` and `GetKanbanBoardHandler`, where one database round-trip is issued per item to resolve blocked status — making the 1000-item backlog <500ms SLA unachievable without a batch fix. The BFS circular-detection and recursive soft-delete cascade methods compound the problem by issuing one DB query per traversal node, which must be replaced with recursive CTEs to stay within acceptable latency at realistic graph depths.

### Unresolved Questions

- No depth or cycle-count cap exists on `GetReachableTargetsAsync`; if the link graph is allowed to grow without bounds (e.g., relates_to chains across projects), the BFS could traverse thousands of nodes. A configurable max-depth guard should be evaluated alongside the CTE rewrite.
- `GetBacklogAsync` (legacy, unpagedmethod, line 41) does not appear to be called by the `GetBacklogHandler` (which uses `GetBacklogPagedAsync`), but its continued presence risks accidental use in future features without pagination or tracking guards.
